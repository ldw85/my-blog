## The Membrane Problem

Before we talk mechanical, let's talk why membrane keyboards frustrate people:

- **No feedback** ‚Äî You press, but can't feel the actuation
- **Fatigue** ‚Äî Harder to type all day
- **Ghosting** ‚Äî Missed keystrokes when typing fast
- **Unreliable** ‚Äî Same keys wear out differently

If you've ever felt "stuck" while typing or had your fingers hurt after a long session ‚Äî it's not you. It's the keyboard.

## Switch Types: The Most Important Choice

This is where most people get stuck. Here's the breakdown:

### Linear (Red/Black) üèÜ Gaming

- **Feel:** Smooth, no bump

- **Best for:** Gaming, fast typing

### Tactile (Brown)

- **Feel:** Bump in the middle
- **Sound:** Moderate
- **Best for:** Balanced use
- **My pick:** Holy Pandas, Boba U4T

- **Feel:** Bump + Click
- **Sound:** **LOUD**
- **Best for:** Satisfaction (and annoying coworkers)

## The "Thocky" Obsession Explained

You've seen YouTubers talking about "thocky" keyboards. What does that even mean?

**Thock = deep, resonant sound**

It's the satisfying "thock" sound when you bottom out the key ‚Äî like knocking on a wooden door.

**How to get it:**
1. Gasket mount (absorbs harsh sounds)
2. PBT keycaps (deeper sound than ABS)
3. Case material (aluminum > plastic)
4. Foam mods (cheap upgrade)

**My honest take:** It sounds like a weird flex, but once you hear it, you get it.

## Common Mistakes I Made

### 1. Buying Before Trying

I spent $200 on a keyboard with Box Jades (clicky) ‚Äî returned it within a week.

**Fix:** Try switches first. Visit a keyboard store or buy samplers.

### 2. Ignoring Keycap Quality

The stock keycaps on my first mechanical keyboard were terrible ‚Äî shine within weeks.

**Fix:** Look for PBT keycaps. GMK is premium but expensive.

### 3. Overbuilding

I spent $400 on my first custom build and didn't even know what I wanted.

**Fix:** Start with a good prebuilt ($100-150). Learn what you like first.
